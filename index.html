Three Structural Dilemmas Under Deep Aging

The Care-for-Rent system addresses core pain points across government, insurance, and social sectors simultaneously.

Government Sector

Aging population pressure mounts while fiscal resources are limited. Traditional institutional care is costly and slow to scale. Home-based care lacks systematic risk monitoring and proactive intervention mechanisms.

Insurance Sector

Home accident claims from solitary elderly remain persistently high. Lack of daily monitoring data prevents accurate actuarial modeling and risk-based pricing for home-based care insurance products.

Social Sector

Youth face urban housing affordability crises while elderly isolation deepens. Existing volunteer-based care models lack sustainability, quality assurance, and scalable incentive structures.

Standardized Care-for-Rent Mutual Assistance Mechanism

This system is a standardized service transaction and supervision mechanism for community mutual elderly care. Eligible youth tenants offset rent with a fixed monthly duration of non-medical daily care services. Through mechanism design and integration of urban stock resources, it can replace 30%–40% of basic caregiver functions and realize proactive intervention of home-based elderly care risks.
